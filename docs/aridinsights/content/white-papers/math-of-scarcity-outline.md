# White Paper Outline: The Math of Scarcity
## Hidden Liabilities in Florida Water Capacity

**Target Audience:** Site Selectors, Portfolio Managers, REIT Executives, Lenders
**Goal:** Establish Arid Insights as the "System of Record" for water risk.
**Tone:** Sober, Mathematical, Institutional, Alerting.

---

## 1. Executive Summary
- **The Thesis:** Water capacity is no longer a utility detail; it is a balance sheet liability.
- **The Shift:** Florida has moved from "supply-side abundance" to "demand-side rationing."
- **The Risk:** Hidden regulatory mechanisms (Sunset Clauses, Chapter 298 assessments, 404 Vacatur) are repricing assets post-closing.
- **The Solution:** Forensic capacity accounting and evidence-first due diligence.

## 2. The Ghost Inventory Problem
- **Definition:** "Ghost Inventory" = Capacity reserved on paper but physically unavailable or legally expired.
- **The Mechanism:** How "Letters of Availability" (LOAs) expire without notice.
- **The Scale:** Estimate of "zombie ERCs" in major metros (Orlando/Tampa).
- **The Asset Impact:** Why your "shovel-ready" site might actually be 18 months from water connectivity.

## 3. The Shadow Tax: Chapter 298 Districts
- **What They Are:** Quasi-governmental "Drainage Districts" with taxation power.
- **The Hidden Lien:** Non-Ad Valorem assessments that don't appear on standard title searches until too late.
- **The Unit Cost:** Case study of assessments reaching $1,000/acre/year (reducing NOI cap rate).
- **The Map:** Why these districts are invisible to standard GIS tools.

## 4. The Regulatory "Hard Stops"
- **404 Vacatur:** The loss of State assumption of wetlands permitting.
    - *Impact:* Timelines moved from 6 months (State) to 24+ months (Federal).
- **SB 64 (Reuse or Die):** The 2032 mandate for zero surface discharge.
    - *Impact:* Utilities are forcing new developments to pay for reclaimed water infrastructure.
- **PFAS Liability:** The coming cost recovery lawsuits for "forever chemicals."

## 5. The Solution: Forensic Capacity Audit
- **Methodology:** 
    1. **Trace:** Don't trust the map; trace the pipe.
    2. **Verify:** Don't trust the LOA; read the rate schedule and meeting minutes.
    3. **Calculate:** Convert "gallons" to "ERCs" (the currency of development).
- **The Arid Insights Approach:** Evidence-lineage for every claim. "If we can't cite the statute, it's an assumption."

## 6. Case Study: The $50,000 Recovery
- **Scenario:** Industrial buyer acquiring a warehousing complex.
- **The Find:** Discovery of expired capacity + overcharged impact fees.
- **The Result:** $50k recovered in capacity credits + avoidance of a $200k lift station upgrade.

## 7. Operational Checklist
- [ ] Has the Letter of Availability been validated against the current Rate Schedule?
- [ ] Is the site within a Chapter 298 District? (Check Tax Roll NAL codes)
- [ ] Is the wetland permit State or Federal jurisdiction?
- [ ] What is the "Sunset Clause" on the capacity reservation?

## Appendix: Key Definitions
- **ERC (Equivalent Residential Connection):** ~300 GPD (varies by utility).
- **CUP (Consumptive Use Permit):** The regulatory ceiling on water withdrawal.
- **ADaPT:** The data standard for water quality reporting.
